About the Park
T.O. Fuller State Park was the first state park open for African Americans east of the Mississippi River, first named Shelby County Negro State Park. It has a new Interpretive Nature and Education Center, hiking trails, playgrounds, an Olympic-size pool and splash pad, ball fields, basketball and tennis courts, and terrific gathering spaces for families, churches, organizations and groups. It has a Chucalissa Indian Village, preserved archaeological excavations, and a modern museum
